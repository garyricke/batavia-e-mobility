# Batavia Safety Squad v2 — "Judy's Family Cafe" Format

*Reformat of the social video series. v1 (`video-scripts.md`) used licensed real crash footage. v2 leans into the Judy's Family Cafe template: an AI-generated, absurd cartoon crash, then a hard cut to a calm Batavia kid on a porch step delivering one rule in deadpan, ending with a near-throwaway "...bataviail dot gov."*

---

## Why this format works

Judy's bit works because the **tonal whiplash IS the joke**. She doesn't sell — she survives. The pancake plug feels almost accidental. People share it because the contrast is delightful.

Applied to safety messaging:

- The **AI crash** is wild, cartoony, impossible. Nobody could mistake it for real. That earns laughs without the ethical baggage of mocking actual injured riders.
- The **live recovery** is calm, almost bored. A real kid on a real Batavia porch. Helmet on. Holding something completely unrelated to the crash (a popsicle, a feather, a tuba).
- The **tip** is delivered like an aside. Not a PSA. Not a lecture. Just a fact, mentioned in passing.
- The **CTA** is the Judy move — undersell it. *"…anyway. Wear your helmet. Bataviail dot gov."* If it sounds like an ad, it dies.

**The character note:** every kid in the Squad is *resigned*. They've survived something insane. They are mildly inconvenienced. They will now tell you the rule. They are not impressed by their own survival.

---

## The Beat Structure (every episode, ~14–18 seconds)

| Time | Beat | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:06 | **AI crash** — Pixar-3D cartoon, increasingly absurd | Slapstick chaos |
| 0:06 | **Hard cut** on impact frame | Audio rug-pull |
| 0:06–0:14 | **Live Squad Kid** — porch / lawn / curb. Real Batavia kid, helmet on, holding a prop from the crash | Deadpan, calm, mildly inconvenienced |
| 0:14–0:16 | **Tip line** (one rule, said like an afterthought) | Throwaway |
| 0:16–0:18 | **Sign-off** — helmet-strap click + "bataviail dot gov" | Undersold |

**Recurring signature:** the helmet-strap **click** is the audio logo. Every episode ends with it. Audiences will start saying it back in the comments — that's how you know it's working.

---

## Recommended AI tools for the crash half

You'll generate the cartoon crashes; the live half you shoot yourselves. Pick a video AI by what each does best:

| Tool | What it's best for | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Sora 2** (OpenAI) | Dynamic physics, exaggerated motion, slapstick crashes | Best for "rocket mode," geese, water splashes. |
| **Veo 3** (Google DeepMind) | Character consistency across cuts, strong cinematography | Use it if you want the same cartoon Squad Kid appearing in multiple crashes before the live cut. Strong audio generation. |
| **Runway Gen-4** | Fast iteration, shorter chaotic scenes | Cheap for B-roll variations and quick re-rolls. |
| **Kling 2.5+** | Affordable, good cartoon style | Workhorse for the longer slapstick beats. |
| **Pika 2.x** | Character motion brushes, controllable cartoon physics | Useful for staging the exact moment of impact for the cut. |

**Style consistency:** every prompt should specify *"Pixar/Disney 3D cartoon style, rounded forms, bright primary colors, exaggerated cartoon physics, no readable text, no logos."* Match the look of the illustrations on the landing page so the print/web/video bundle reads as one campaign.

**Goal:** Cold-launch the format. No meta intro. The bit IS the introduction.

**The AI Crash** (6s, 9:16)

> *Prompt:* A cinematic wide of a quiet sunny Batavia suburban street. ## Production notes

**For the AI crash half:**

- Generate multiple takes per crash and pick the funniest physics. Cartoon AI is unpredictable — that's a feature.
- Lock the **Pixar/Disney 3D cartoon** style in every prompt. Consistency across episodes is what makes this a *series*.
- Aim for 6 seconds per crash, maximum. Vertical 9:16, 1080×1920.
- Bake the title card (e.g., "GEESE GOT ME") on screen in **Bagel Fat One** font for the first 1.5 seconds — same font as the landing page.

**For the live recovery half:**

- Shoot vertical, natural light, golden hour or early morning. Batavia downtown brick, the Fox River footbridges, and tree-lined residential streets all work.
- The kid must already be in position when the cut happens — sitting up, brushing off, mildly inconvenienced. **Do not show them falling.** They were already on the ground when we got there.
- One take, in character. The bit dies if it feels rehearsed.
- Helmet must be visibly on for the entire shot. The helmet sticker (small star) is a small but valuable continuity detail.
- Burn captions in. Bagel Fat One for emphasis words, sans-serif for body. Stop-red highlight on the rule keyword. TikTok and Reels both default to muted playback.

**Posting cadence & cross-posting:**

- One episode per Tuesday for 12 weeks (Pilot + 10 + finale).
- Post simultaneously to TikTok, Reels, and Shorts.
- Cross-promote each Tuesday morning from the City of Batavia and Batavia PD official accounts.
- Pin the safety guide URL in every bio.

**Measurement:**

- UTM-tag the bio link per platform: `?utm_source=tiktok&utm_campaign=safety-squad&utm_content=ep01-geese`.
- The KPI is **landing-page sessions from social**, not video views. Views are downstream; clicks are the proof.
- Set a Google Analytics 4 event for clicks on the "Read the law" outbound links from the landing page — those clicks tell you which parents got curious enough to dig.

**Things to NOT do:**

- Don't have the kid look excited about the crash. They survived. They're tired. They're eating a popsicle now.
- Don't have the kid say "Hi guys!" or "What's up!" or any influencer opener. The bit starts mid-thought.
- Don't make the CTA the climax. The CTA is the afterthought. The rule is the climax — and even that's said like a fun fact.
- Don't show a real kid actually falling. Ever. The cartoon takes ALL the impact. The live half is always post-impact.
- Don't use real brand names, license plates, or readable street signs in the AI prompts. Generators sometimes try to invent these and the results are uncanny.
