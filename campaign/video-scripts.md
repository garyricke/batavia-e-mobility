# Batavia Safety Squad — Video Script Series

*A 12-episode short-form social series for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Each video uses an existing e-mobility crash clip as a hook, hard-cuts to a Batavia kid "getting up" from the moment of impact, delivers one rule from Ordinance 2026-010, and ends with a call to action to visit the safety guide.*

---

## Series concept

**Format:** 9:16 vertical, 12–18 seconds total.

**Beat structure (every episode):**

1. **0:00–0:04 — The Fail.** Open on the existing crash clip from the moment things start to go wrong.
2. **0:04 — Smash cut on impact.** Same frame size, same lighting direction — the cut should feel like the same camera kept rolling.
3. **0:04–0:13 — The Squad Kid.** A Batavia kid in the matching pose (lying down, half-in-bushes, sliding to a stop) sits up, dusts off, delivers the tip directly into the camera.
4. **0:13–0:16 — The Stamp.** A red "BATAVIA SAFETY TIP #__" stamp slams onto the frame with a wet-ink sound. Card text: **"Get the full guide → bataviail.gov/e-mobility-guide"**.
5. **0:16 — Helmet click.** Every kid signs off with the same beat: tap helmet, click strap, point at camera. This is the series's audio + visual signature.

**Recurring elements (build the brand across the series):**

- A **rotating cast of 4–5 kids** plays "The Safety Squad" across all episodes. Same kids = recognizable series.
- Every helmet has the same **small star sticker** (cheap, fun, on-brand with the guide page).
- **Caption font** on-screen: *Bagel Fat One* (the chunky display font used on the landing page) — instant visual link.
- **Color palette** matches the guide: cream paper, ink navy, marigold yellow, stop red.
- **End card** is identical every time so the CTA becomes muscle memory for the audience.

**One practical heads-up on rights:** scraping random TikToks for the crash footage can get you DMCA'd or worse. Safer routes — pick one or mix: (a) license clips through a stock-fail provider, (b) get explicit DM permission from creators and credit them on the card, (c) recreate the crashes yourselves with stunt mats and a willing teenager (often the funniest option anyway). The Squad-Kid reaction half is always your own footage, which keeps that half clean.

# The Scripts

## Pilot — "Meet the Squad"

*Sets up the series. Run first, then pin to profile.*

- **Open:** Whip-pan montage of 4 Batavia kids lined up against a brick wall downtown, each in a different colored helmet, arms crossed, sunglasses-down energy.
- **Kid #1 (to camera):** "We're the Batavia Safety Squad."
- **Kid #2:** "We watch the gnarliest e-bike fails on the internet…"
- **Kid #3:** "…so you don't have to be the next one."
- **Kid #4:** "Twelve tips. One rule each. Let's ride."
- *All four click their helmet straps in unison.*
- **End card:** "BATAVIA SAFETY SQUAD — Tips drop every Tuesday. Full guide at bataviail.gov."

---

## #1 — "Helmet Hero"

**Maps to:** Safety Rule #1 — anyone under 16 must wear a helmet on any e-mobility device.

- **Source clip:** Classic e-bike face-plant where the helmet visibly takes the hit.
- **The cut:** On impact frame.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid lying flat on grass next to a tipped-over bike, dirt on one cheek, helmet slightly crooked.
- **Kid (sitting up, grinning):** *"Saw that? Yeah. THAT'S why I wear this."* *(taps helmet)* *"Under sixteen in Batavia? Helmet. Every ride. Even just to your friend's house. That's the rule."*
- **Card:** **RULE #1 — Under 16, helmet on. Every. Single. Ride.**

---

## #2 — "Sidewalk Slam"

**Maps to:** Where to Ride — e-bikes are not allowed on sidewalks (any class).

- **Source clip:** E-bike clipping a pedestrian or trash can on a sidewalk.
- **The cut:** On impact frame.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid sprawled across the sidewalk next to a fallen e-bike, a confused (actor) walker frozen mid-step holding a dog leash.
- **Kid (popping up):** *"My bad! E-bikes — ANY class — don't ride sidewalks in Batavia."* *(points to bike lane)* *"That green paint? That's our lane. Sidewalks are for walking."*
- **Card:** **WHERE TO RIDE — E-bikes belong in the bike lane.**

---

## #3 — "The 28-MPH Wake-Up"

**Maps to:** Class 3 e-bikes — operator must be 16+ AND wear a helmet (every rider, no exceptions).

- **Source clip:** High-speed e-bike crash where the rider eats it hard.
- **The cut:** On impact, audio drops out.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid (older — like 14 or 15) sitting in a hedge, blinking slowly.
- **Kid (deadpan):** *"That was Class 3. Twenty-eight miles an hour. You wanna ride one of those in Batavia? Cool. Be sixteen. Wear the helmet. Every. Single. Time. Not negotiable."*
- **Card:** **CLASS 3 E-BIKES — 16+. Helmet always.**

---

## #4 — "Lights or Lights Out"

**Maps to:** Safety Rule #2 — front white light + rear red light, each visible 500 ft after dark.

- **Source clip:** Nighttime crash where an unlit rider gets clipped or doesn't see a curb.
- **The cut:** On impact, screen briefly goes black.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid sitting on the curb at dusk. A bright white headlight on his bike glows in the foreground. He flips on a red taillight with his thumb.
- **Kid:** *"Sun's down? You need this…"* *(points at headlight)* *"…and this."* *(points at taillight)* *"White in front. Red in back. Visible from five hundred feet. That's the law."*
- **Card:** **AFTER DARK — Front white + rear red. 500 ft.**

---

## #5 — "That's Not An E-Bike"

**Maps to:** E-motos — over 750 W, throttle past 20 mph, no working pedals = banned on every public street, sidewalk, path, and park.

- **Source clip:** Dirt-bike-style "e-bike" (i.e., a Surron or similar) crashing on a street or park path.
- **The cut:** On impact.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid (looking unimpressed) standing next to the crashed red e-moto with arms crossed.
- **Kid:** *"That's not an e-bike. That's an e-moto. Over seven-fifty watts, throttle past twenty. Banned in Batavia — streets, sidewalks, paths, parks, all of it. Private property only, parents."*
- **Card:** **PARENTS — If it looks like a dirt bike, it's an e-moto.**

---

## #6 — "One-Hand Wonder"

**Maps to:** Safety Rule — keep at least one hand on the handlebars; don't carry stuff that impairs your grip.

- **Source clip:** Rider holding phone / drink / bag with both hands crashing.
- **The cut:** On impact.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid lying in a pile of leaves, still clutching a now-cracked iced coffee.
- **Kid:** *"Phone in one hand. Drink in the other. Steering with my CHEEKS apparently. Don't be me. At least ONE hand on the bars. Always."*
- **Card:** **RULE — One hand on the bars. Minimum.**

---

## #7 — "No Sidewalk Speedsters"

**Maps to:** Class 2 e-scooters (up to 20 mph) — not on sidewalks. Class 1 (≤10 mph) is OK on sidewalks.

- **Source clip:** Fast stand-up scooter on a sidewalk wiping out near pedestrians.
- **The cut:** On impact.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid skidding to a stop on a sidewalk, scooter on its side.
- **Kid:** *"Class 2 scooter — twenty miles an hour — on a sidewalk? Nope. Twenty belongs in the street with the bikes. Sidewalks are for the SLOW kind. Know what class you're on."*
- **Card:** **CLASS 2 SCOOTERS — Not on sidewalks.**

---

## #8 — "The Yield"

**Maps to:** Safety Rule — always yield to pedestrians.

- **Source clip:** Rider barreling through a crosswalk or path and clipping a walker.
- **The cut:** On impact.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid sitting on the bike path, the pedestrian (actor) helping her up.
- **Kid:** *"Pedestrian on the path? You YIELD. Even if you have the right of way. Even if you're late. Even if your friend just texted. Yield. Every time."*
- **Card:** **RULE — Walkers always win.**

---

## #9 — "The Tampered Throttle"

**Maps to:** Safety Rule — no removing speed governors, no modifying your device past legal specs.

- **Source clip:** Clearly souped-up e-bike doing way too much and crashing.
- **The cut:** On impact.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid sitting next to a smoking, clearly hacked bike with extra batteries duct-taped on.
- **Kid (mock-impressed):** *"Took off the speed limiter. Added a second battery. Welded on some flames. Congrats — that's not an e-bike anymore. That's a fine. A real one. With your name on it."*
- **Card:** **RULE — Don't tamper. Factory-fast is plenty fast.**

---

## #10 — "Mom Pays"

**Maps to:** Fines — $50 / $100 / $500 / $750+. Parents responsible for minors' violations.

- **Source clip:** A teen on an obvious e-moto getting absolutely sent and crashing.
- **The cut:** On impact.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid lying flat, holding up a phone with "MOM CALLING" on the screen.
- **Kid (counting on fingers):** *"First time, fifty bucks. Second, a hundred. Third, five hundred. Fourth?"* *(winces)* *"Seven-fifty MINIMUM. And guess who pays for everything?"* *(phone rings louder)* *"…Yeah."*
- **Card:** **FINES STACK. Parents are on the hook.**

---

## #11 — "Wrong Road"

**Maps to:** E-scooters and micromobility — not on state highways, not on roads posted above 35 mph.

- **Source clip:** Scooter rider on a busy main road or arterial nearly getting hit.
- **The cut:** On impact (just the close call, no graphic stuff).
- **Open on:** Squad Kid standing on the shoulder of a fast road, looking up at a speed limit sign.
- **Kid:** *"Posted over thirty-five? State highway? That's a hard NO on your scooter or skateboard. Find the bike path. The cars want this road. We don't want it back."*
- **Card:** **SCOOTERS — Not on 35+ mph roads or state highways.**

---

## #12 — "The Age Check"

**Maps to:** Stand-up e-scooters require 18+. Class 2 micromobility 18+. Class 1 micromobility 13+.

- **Source clip:** A clearly young kid riding an adult-sized stand-up scooter and crashing.
- **The cut:** On impact.
- **Open on:** Squad Kid sitting next to the crashed scooter, then hopping onto an electric skateboard instead.
- **Kid:** *"Stand-up e-scooter? Eighteen-plus in Illinois. I'm twelve. So I'm on this."* *(rides off on a Class 1 board)* *"Know your age. Know your ride. Both matter."*
- **Card:** **AGE LIMITS — E-scooter 18+. Class 1 micromobility 13+.**

---

## Outro / Season finale — "Blooper Reel"

*Run last. Builds goodwill and reuses the best behind-the-scenes moments.*

- **Beats:** Squad members repeatedly fake-crashing into hay bales. A helmet falls off mid-line. Two kids accidentally collide on scooters and laugh. A dog photobombs the take. The director (off camera) saying "ONE MORE."
- **Final beat:** All four kids lined up again, helmets on. They click their straps in sync. One looks at camera: *"Real rules. Real Batavia. Get the guide."*
- **End card:** **bataviail.gov/e-mobility-guide** — held longer than usual (3 seconds).

## Notes for the creative team

**Casting:** The four hero illustrations on the landing page were designed with a deliberately diverse cast (Black girl in pink, Latino boy in marigold, Asian girl in pink, etc.). Mirror that mix in casting so the videos feel of a piece with the printed/web guide. Bonus: dress the cast in helmet colors that match the illustrations on the landing page so the cross-media link is immediate.

**Tone calibration:** These should feel like the kids are in on the joke, not being talked at. The crash-fail clips do the comedy work. The kid's line is the payoff. Avoid PSA voice — let them speak like kids actually speak. Light improv on the lines is good; the rule has to land verbatim.

**Captions:** Burn captions in. TikTok defaults to muted playback and kids/parents both consume that way. Use the same Bagel Fat One display font from the guide for emphasis words, and a standard sans for body. Stop-red highlight on the rule number.

**Posting cadence:** One episode per week for 13 weeks (pilot + 12 + blooper). Cross-post to TikTok, Reels, and Shorts. Bio link points to the landing page.

**Hashtag set:** `#BataviaSafetySquad #RideSmartRideSafe #ebikesafety #emobility #BataviaIL`. Pin the safety guide URL in the profile bio.

**Measurement:** Track UTM-tagged clicks from the bio to the landing page (e.g., `?utm_source=tiktok&utm_campaign=safety-squad`). The Squad's job is to drive curious riders to the full guide — episode views are nice, but landing-page sessions from social is the real KPI.
