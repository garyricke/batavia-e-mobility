# Communications Plan: “Roll Smart” Campaign

## I. Campaign Foundation 

**Core Content Foundations**  
**To be included in website, flyers, social posts, event talking points, PD messaging**

* What devices are (and aren’t) allowed 

* Where you can ride 

* Age requirements 

* Safety rules 

* Fines and consequences 

## II. Messaging Strategy (Aligned to Ordinance)

**Primary Campaign Message: Roll Smart: Know the rules. Ride safe. Respect others.**

**1\. Know Your Ride**

* Not all devices follow the same rules 

* Age limits matter (especially scooters \= 18+) 

**2\. Know Where to Ride**

* Sidewalk vs. street vs. trail rules vary 

* E-bikes ≠ sidewalks 

* Faster devices \= more restrictions 

**3\. Ride Safe**

* Helmets under 16 

* Lights at night 

* No reckless riding 

**4\. Know the Consequences**

* Fines escalate quickly 

* Devices can be impounded 

* Parents can be held responsible 

## III. Content Channels  

**A. Website (Primary Hub)**

**Sections:**

* Device types 

* Where you can ride 

* Safety rules 

* Fines & penalties 

* FAQ 

Include:

* “Quick Answers for Parents” 

* “Quick Rules for Riders” 

**B. Printed Materials** 

**1\. Main Flyer (Parent-Focused)**  
Headline:  
**“Before They Ride, Know the Rules”**

* Age requirements 

* Where kids can ride 

* Parent responsibility clause

Front:  
**Roll Smart Basics**

* No sidewalks (e-bikes) 

* Scooters \= 18+ 

* Helmets under 16 

* Lights at night 

Back:

* Fine structure (simple) 

* QR \+ URL 

**3\. Youth Handout**  
Headline:  
**“Don’t Get Fined. Roll Smart.”**

* 3–5 rules max 

* Visual icons 

* Peer tone 

**C. Social Media Content**

**Weekly Content Breakdown** 

**Week 1: What Counts as an E-Mobility Device**

* Break down device types

* Sidewalk vs street confusion 

* Trail etiquette 

**Week 3: Age Rules**

* Scooter \= 18+ 

* Class 3 e-bike \= 16+ 

**Week 4: Safety Rules**

* Helmets, lights, behavior 

**Week 5: What Happens If You Don’t Follow the Rules**

* Fines 

* Impoundment 

* Parent responsibility 

**D. Video Integration** 

**Short, scenario-based videos**:

**Video Concepts (Directly from Your Guide)**

**1\. “Sidewalk or Street?”**

* Show correct vs incorrect riding locations 

**2\. “Can You Ride This?”**

* Age-based scenarios (teen \+ scooter \= no) 

**3\. “What Happens If You Break the Rules?”**

* Police explaining fines in plain language 

**4\. “Night Ride Check”**

* Lights \+ visibility demo 

## IV. Event Messaging-talking points

At Farmers Market / events, staff should consistently say:

**3 Key Talking Points:**

1. “Not all devices follow the same rules” 

2. “Where you ride depends on what you’re riding” 

3. “Parents can be responsible for violations” 

This keeps messaging consistent across departments.

## V. Police Department Integration 

(Use BPD written guide)

* Training reference for officers 

* Handout during stops/warnings 

* Script for community conversations 

**Phased Approach**

* May–June: Education \+ warnings 

* July–August: Increased enforcement messaging 

## 

## **VI. Risk & Clarity Considerations** 

The BPD written guide includes **important legal clarity points** that should be emphasized:

* **Scooters \= 18+ (state law alignment)** 

* **E-motos are fully banned** 

* **Parent liability exists** 

These are the **highest-risk confusion areas** → prioritize in messaging.

## VII. Themes

Campaign broken out into four themes throughout the summer—though all messaging should refer to webpage for full details about the rules. This will create consistency in messaging on social, in flyers, events, newsletter

**“Roll Smart Rules” Series**

* Rule \#1: Know Your Device – May

* Rule \#2: Ride in the Right Place  –  June

* Rule \#3: Follow Safety Rules  – July

* Rule \#4: Know the Consequences \--August

* Finalize messaging

(Content development to be handled by Comms manager with input and final approval by ATAC and PD)

* Build webpage  
* Create quick rule card; flyer (one parent-focused, one youth focused)  
* Create lawn signs (if desired)  
* Write first 4 to 6 weeks of social media content  
* Draft press release  
* Script and produce videos

### May 15- June15 (Heavy education push with goal to build awareness of rules)

* Launch Roll Smart webpage  
* Issue press release  
* Publish kickoff social post  
* Distribute printed pieces (at bike event Saturday, May 16, school backpacks, library, park district, city hall; place lawn signs

June 16-July (reinforcement and behavior shift focus to deepen understanding and influence behavior

### Aug 1-14 (Back to school push and wrap up)

* Youth-focused reminders for how to get to school safely  
* Posts about top five rules to remember  
* Recap video and post of entire series in one place  
* Video, social posts, newsletter, homepage content thanking community for participating and reminding folks to drive carefully during school commuting hours
